It continues the story of the Four Horsemen, a team of illusionists who use their skills to expose corruption and pull off daring heists. <h2 class="wp-block-heading">Plot</h2>
<p>The Four Horsemen return after years in hiding. Their reputation as vigilante illusionists has made them legends. But a new villain, a tech billionaire, manipulates them into performing impossible heists across the world.</p>
<p>The team must steal advanced technology while staying ahead of law enforcement and rival magicians. Each trick is bigger, riskier, and more dangerous. Along the way, secrets from their past resurface, testing their trust in one another.</p>
<p>The climax unfolds in Shanghai, where the Horsemen must pull off their greatest illusion to expose corruption and reclaim their freedom. The film balances dazzling magic sequences with emotional stakes, showing that loyalty is the greatest trick of all.</p>
<h2 class="wp-block-heading">Cast</h2>
<p>Jesse Eisenberg reprises his role as J. Daniel Atlas, the confident leader of the Horsemen who struggles with control and trust. Woody Harrelson returns as Merritt McKinney, the mentalist whose humor hides deep loyalty. Dave Franco plays Jack Wilder, the street magician who proves himself as a skilled illusionist. Lizzy Caplan appears again as Lula May, the bold newcomer who adds flair and unpredictability to the group. Mark Ruffalo plays Dylan Rhodes, the FBI agent turned mentor who guides the Horsemen while facing his own demons. Benedict Cumberbatch joins the cast as Adrian Black, the tech mogul antagonist who manipulates the team. Other supporting roles include international actors portraying rival magicians, law enforcement officers, and mysterious allies.</p>
<h2 class="wp-block-heading">Final Words</h2>
<p><em>Now You See Me: Now You Don&rsquo;t</em>&nbsp;(2025) is a thrilling continuation of the illusionist heist saga.
